About Taylor Made

Taylor was a boy with deep roots. Growing up in a small, close-knit town, he made friends easily, was the first to volunteer when someone needed help, and genuinely loved his community. Known for his kindness and gentle humor, he'd become a fixture in his town—a familiar face at the corner store, a regular at the community center, and a mentor to younger kids on the baseball team.

One sunny Saturday at a town event, Taylor met a girl named Lily. She was new to the area, with a radiant smile and a warm laugh that caught his attention right away. He walked over and introduced himself, his heart pounding a little faster than usual. They chatted, finding they shared a love for old books, campfires, and chocolate chip cookies. As the evening ended, he asked if he could take her out on a proper date, and she agreed with a smile.

Their first date was simple—a walk around the lake and ice cream from a local stand. When Taylor walked her to her door, Lily gave him a warm hug goodnight, lingering just long enough to make his heart flutter but short enough to leave him wanting more.

This became their routine. Every date ended with Lily's hug, and each hug felt a little closer, a little warmer, but still ended too soon. Over the months, Taylor began to imagine those hugs turning into something more permanent. Finally, on a quiet evening under the stars, Taylor asked Lily to be his wife. She said yes, and soon enough, the whole town was buzzing about their upcoming wedding.

On their wedding day, Taylor stood at the altar, his heart swelling with anticipation as Lily walked down the aisle. Their family and friends were there, smiling and teary-eyed, witnessing the union of two beloved souls. And when the pastor finally said, "You may now kiss the bride," Taylor didn't waste a moment. He kissed her softly, then deeply, and then time seemed to stop. Their family and friends began to chuckle, the chuckles turned into full laughter, then quieted, only to start all over again. The long awaited kiss didn't seem to have an end.

Finally, Taylor pulled back, slightly sheepish, and glanced at the pastor. The pastor raised an eyebrow with a grin and said, “Well, it looks like Taylor here has planted a kiss as deep as his roots!”

The whole congregation burst into laughter again, and Taylor, still blushing, couldn't help but laugh too. He squeezed Lily's hand, knowing that he had found a love worth waiting for.
